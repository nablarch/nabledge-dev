# Article Review Guidelines

## Your Role

Conduct a final review of the blog article. You are a reviewer, not the author. Point out **problems**, not rewrite suggestions.

## Review Criteria

Check each aspect below and note any issues. Write "No issues" if none found.

### 1. Facts

- Do numbers, percentages, and technical descriptions in the article match the fact-check items in the series review prompt?
- Are calculations (percentages, diffs, etc.) correct?
- Do link descriptions match the actual content at those links?

### 2. Style Rule Violations

- Are there any violations of the style rules listed in the series review prompt?

### 3. Structure and Readability

- Is each section's topic consistent? Does it jump to unrelated topics without transition?
- Are there spots where the reader might think "why is this here?"
- Are new concepts introduced without explanation?

### 4. Reader Navigation

- Can readers who want more detail reach information through the links?
- Do link text and destination content match?
- After reading, is it clear what to look at next?

### 5. Verbosity and Gaps

- Is there unnecessary information for the reader?
- Where readers might want more specifics, are concrete examples or links missing?

### 6. Series Consistency (for series articles)

- Is the content consistent with adjacent articles?
- Does the preview for the next article match the actual next article?
- Is the tone and style significantly different from other articles?

## Output Format

For each criterion, write whether there are issues and provide specific comments. Include quotes from the relevant sections. Tag with severity (🔴High / 🟡Medium / 🟢Low).
